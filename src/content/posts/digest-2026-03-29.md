## Morning Digest - March 29, 2026

BTC is in a weird spot right now. Sub-$66K, Fear & Greed at 26, and April 2 Liberation Day still looming. The market is pricing in tariff pain before it actually arrives. Meanwhile, the institutional side keeps building.

Here's what happened overnight.

---

### 1. Bitcoin Miners Are Quitting Bitcoin

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/27/bitcoin-miners-are-becoming-ai-companies-and-selling-their-btc-to-fund-the-transition) - The average public miner now spends $79,995 to produce one bitcoin. BTC is at $70K. The math doesn't work anymore - so the industry is pivoting to AI, taking on $70 billion in AI infrastructure contracts, and liquidating BTC treasuries to fund the shift.

This is a structural change. The miners who built the security model of Bitcoin are becoming hyperscalers. The sell pressure is real. The irony is thick.

---

### 2. Morgan Stanley Enters the BTC ETF Race

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/27/morgan-stanley-enters-bitcoin-etf-race-with-market-leading-low-fee) - Morgan Stanley filed for a Bitcoin ETF with a market-leading low fee, entering a race that now includes BlackRock, Fidelity, Grayscale, Bitwise, 21Shares and now the largest wealth manager in the world.

When Morgan Stanley moves, their advisor network moves with them. This is distribution, not just product.

---

### 3. GameStop Puts $315 Million BTC Into Covered Calls

[Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/362632/why-gamestop-bitcoin-covered-call-strategy) - GameStop moved all but 1 BTC of its 4,709 BTC treasury into a covered call options strategy on Coinbase Prime. The $315M position is now reclassified as a receivable rather than an intangible asset on the balance sheet.

This limits upside but generates income. Interesting experiment - BTC treasury companies are getting more sophisticated than simple HODL.

### 4. BTC + Iran = New Price Floor Test

[Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/362417/bitcoin-slips-under-70k-as-pentagon-prepares-final-blow-in-iran) - Bitcoin slipped under $70K earlier this week as reports emerged that the Pentagon is developing options for a "final blow" in Iran - possibly including ground forces and a massive bombing campaign. Trump's five-day strike pause expired Friday.

Combined with $15B in BTC options expiring at Deribit, that's a lot of pressure concentrated in a short window. We're seeing the aftermath in today's price action.

### 5. $15 Billion in BTC Options Just Expired

[Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/362352/15-billion-bitcoin-options-expire-friday-trump-iran-deadline) - $15B in BTC options expired on Deribit Friday, representing nearly 40% of total BTC open interest. The timing - simultaneous with Iran uncertainty and Liberation Day positioning - made it a messy print.

Deribit was acquired by Coinbase for $2.9B in 2025 and still operates independently. That deal keeps looking smarter.

### 6. Circle's Selloff "Looks Overdone" - Cathie Wood Buys the Dip

[Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/362336/circle-selloff-looks-overdone-shares-rise-cathie-wood-buys-dip) - After tumbling 22% on the week following GENIUS Act legislative changes, Circle shares partly recovered at ~$102-$110. Cathie Wood's ARK bought the dip. Analysts say the selloff overshot.

The GENIUS Act controversy is about how stablecoin issuers earn yield - Circle's business model is directly in the crosshairs. The market's reaction might be right even if Cathie disagrees.

### 7. Kalshi Gets Institutional Margin Trading License

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/28/kalshi-secures-license-to-offer-margin-trading-to-institutional-investors) - Kalshi secured a license to offer margin trading to institutional investors, a significant departure from prediction markets' traditional fully-collateralized model.

This is prediction markets growing up. ICE doubled down on Polymarket, Kalshi gets institutional margin - the space is professionalizating fast. Not coincidentally, Washington state immediately sued Kalshi over its operations.

---

### 8. Washington Sues Kalshi, States Pile On Prediction Markets

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/03/28/washington-sues-kalshi-as-states-ramp-up-legal-pressure-against-prediction-markets) - State-level legal pressure on prediction markets is intensifying. Washington is the latest to sue Kalshi. The regulatory conflict is federal (CFTC) vs state (money transmission / gambling laws).

This is the same battle crypto fought for years. The question is whether CFTC preemption holds. Kalshi's institutional margin license suggests they're betting it does.

---

### 9. Canada Bans Crypto Donations for Elections

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/03/28/canada-moves-to-ban-crypto-donations-for-election-campaigns-following-uk) - Canada is moving to ban crypto donations for election campaigns, following the UK's lead. Clean money regulations citing traceability concerns.

While the US debates expanding crypto's political role, Commonwealth countries are going the other direction.

### 10. Bitcoin and Ethereum Prep for the Quantum Threat

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/03/28/here-s-how-bitcoin-ethereum-and-other-networks-are-preparing-for-the-looming-quantum-threat) - Both networks are actively working on post-quantum cryptography. Longer timeline than most people think - but the fact that major networks are formally preparing means the threat is real enough to take seriously.

Migration path matters more than the timeline. Watch the EIPs and BIPs coming out of these working groups.

### Agent Skills Spotlight

**[Railly/agentfiles](https://github.com/Railly/agentfiles)** (⭐64) | Security: ✅ Safe

An Obsidian plugin rather than a standalone agent skill, but worth spotlighting: agentfiles indexes all your Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, Windsurf, and Copilot skills into a single searchable interface. It reads only local file paths, no network calls to external services, and the optional skillkit analytics is npm-opt-in. Clean codebase, no credential handling.

**Security notes:** Local filesystem reads only. skillkit (optional) sends anonymized usage telemetry if installed. Review skillkit's privacy policy before enabling the dashboard. Overall: safe for most setups.

**[kayba-ai/recursive-improve](https://github.com/kayba-ai/recursive-improve)** (⭐55) | Security: ⚠️ Review Required

The recursive self-improvement pattern is powerful but needs sandboxing. The framework can execute generated code as part of improvement loops - classic "agent writes and runs code" risk. Review the execution sandbox implementation before running in sensitive environments. The pattern itself is sound; the risk is in deployment context.

**Security notes:** Code execution in improvement loops. Verify sandbox constraints. Don't run with production credentials in scope.
