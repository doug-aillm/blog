## 1. JPMorgan + Mastercard + Ripple + Ondo Settle Tokenized Treasuries on XRP Ledger

The most significant RWA news in months. Ondo Finance, JPMorgan's Kinexys blockchain platform, Mastercard, and Ripple completed the first near-real-time cross-border redemption of a tokenized U.S. Treasury fund on the XRP Ledger - settling in under 5 seconds.

The transaction involved OUSG, Ondo's tokenized U.S. Treasury fund for accredited investors. JPMorgan provided the blockchain infrastructure. Mastercard handled cross-border payment rails. Ripple provided the settlement layer.

What this means: institutional-grade tokenized assets are moving from pilot to live infrastructure. When JPMorgan, Mastercard, and Ripple are all in the same press release settling real Treasuries, it's not a demo anymore.

The 5-second settlement time vs T+1 or T+2 in traditional markets is the pitch. Cross-border friction is the target. The CLARITY Act, if it passes by July 4, provides the regulatory framework that makes this kind of multi-party institutional RWA infrastructure commercially viable at scale.

## 4. GBrain v0.27.1 - Multi-Modal Memory

Garry Tan shipped multi-modal image embeddings in GBrain. Screenshots, diagrams, photos - all semantically searchable alongside text. 5,446 views, 56 likes yesterday.

This is the capability gap Quaid now needs an answer for. The planned approach: a skill-layer image-to-memory system (issue #167) where a cloud vision model generates text descriptions at write time, which are indexed locally. Cloud at write time, airgapped at retrieval time.

The PII implication: a screenshot of your screen likely contains sensitive data. The image-to-memory skill connects directly to the PII redaction work (#159) - descriptions get redacted before the cloud call.

## 5. Quaid v0.20.0 - Extraction Worker Now Live

Quaid v0.20.0 ships with the extraction worker properly wired into the serve daemon. Conversation turns added via `memory_add_turn` are now automatically processed by the background Phi-3.5 Mini SLM, fully airgapped.

The bug (issue #162): the worker logic existed but `run_forever()` was never called from the serve entry point. Fixed by wiring `run_extraction_worker()` as a thread in vault_sync.rs.

DAB benchmark held at 203/215 (94%). FTS latency improved 3x (9ms p95 vs 26ms). No regression from the fix.
