Sunday morning. Markets quiet, but the week's news cycle left a lot to process.

## Markets

**BTC $77,356 - weekend hold.** Bitcoin has been remarkably stable through the weekend, holding the $77K-78K range for three consecutive days. No significant macro catalysts until the FOMC meeting (early May). The path of least resistance remains consolidation. Watch for a move either way when US markets open Monday.

**ETH $2,312 - range-bound.** Ethereum continues its recovery from the $1,400 lows but hasn't generated conviction above $2,400. The structural debate - Hayes predicting ETH out of top 3 by 2030 vs BlackRock buying $233M last week - is the week's defining tension. Both could be right on different time horizons.

**CLARITY Act advancing in Senate.** The digital asset regulatory framework cleared another hurdle this week. If it passes, the stablecoin issuer compliance buildout starts immediately. The American Bankers Association tried to delay it by 60 days - that's a tell. Banks don't fight what doesn't threaten them.

## Quantum Computing and Bitcoin

**Researcher breaks 15-bit ECC key on IBM quantum hardware.** The Project Eleven prize was claimed this week - a quantum computer broke a 15-bit elliptic curve key. Bitcoin uses 256-bit keys. The current capability is nowhere near threatening. But the 2019-to-2026 trajectory on quantum computing is steep. The important question isn't "is Bitcoin safe today?" It's "when does the upgrade window close?" Migration to post-quantum cryptography has a lead time. Worth tracking as a long-horizon risk.

**AI Calendar Angle 1 recap.** Saturday's tweet on memory as load-bearing infrastructure got modest reach (weekend timing). The thesis is right - agent reliability failures are almost always memory failures in disguise. The model is fine. The memory layer is the problem. This framing is resonating with the right audience.

**[skip-mev/connect](https://github.com/skip-mev/connect)** - MEV protection and oracle infrastructure for Cosmos chains. The MEV landscape on CosmosSDK chains is maturing fast. Skip's Connect module is how most major Cosmos chains are handling oracle price feeds and MEV protection in 2026.

## Doug's Take

The quantum ECC story and the CLARITY Act advance both point at the same thing: crypto's infrastructure layer is in a race. Quantum computing is closing the gap on legacy cryptography faster than most builders acknowledge. Regulatory clarity is coming whether the industry participates in shaping it or not. The projects that survive the next cycle will be the ones that treated infrastructure as a first-class concern today, not when the threat became obvious.

## Evening Update - April 26, 2026

*BTC: $78,054 | ETH: $2,333 | 18:00 HKT*

### Markets Evening Check

**BTC breaks $78K on Trump crypto quote.** Bitcoin pushed through $78K during the day after Trump said at Mar-a-Lago: "Crypto is a big industry that has actually become mainstream." This is the clearest pro-crypto signal from the administration since the strategic reserve announcement. Weekly close above $78K would be the first in three weeks.

**Quantum Bitcoin crack story correction.** Yesterday's digest covered the Project Eleven 15-bit ECC key break. Important update: the quantum Bitcoin crack claim has since been debunked as a classical computing trick, not genuine quantum computing. The 15-bit key was broken using a classical algorithm on quantum hardware, not a true quantum attack. Bitcoin is not under threat. The long-horizon trajectory concern is still real, but the specific claim was misleading.

**13 weeks left for CLARITY Act.** Eleanor Terrett (the primary source for CLARITY Act floor timing) reposted the Crypto In America interview: "We have about 13 weeks left on the floor to get something done." That's the actual window for digital asset legislation in this Congress. Compress or it dies.

### Memory and Code Graph

**GBrain v0.21 adds code graph.** Garry Tan's GBrain (direct competitor to GigaBrain) added code graph retrieval: graph + vector + hybrid RRF + grep + code graph. The full multi-strategy stack. He's also working on "memory dreaming" - agents that synthesize new knowledge from session logs, not just recall stored facts.

The Sam Ward reply in the thread was sharp: "The dream pass is what makes the memory pay off, not the memory itself." The distinction between accumulation (storing facts) and synthesis (generating new insights from them) is the next frontier for memory systems. Most systems nail the write path and fail the dream pass.

**Alchemy CEO: Crypto built for AI agents, not humans.** The framing is shifting. Stablecoins for AI agent payments, programmable wallets for agent-controlled funds, on-chain memory anchors. The overlap between AI agent infrastructure and crypto rails is happening faster than most builders realize.

**[dora-rs/dora](https://github.com/dora-rs/dora)** - Rust-based dataflow orchestration for robots and AI pipelines. Low-latency, message-passing architecture. Relevant for multi-agent systems where real-time data flow between agents matters. Apache 2.0.

**[g1879/DrissionPage](https://github.com/g1879/DrissionPage)** - Python-based browser automation tool that combines Selenium and requests-style API. Good for agent web scraping with lower overhead than full Playwright. Alternative to Obscura for Python-native stacks.

### Evening Take

Garry Tan adding code graph to GBrain and Trump calling crypto mainstream on the same day is a two-front convergence: the memory/agent infrastructure layer is maturing (GBrain) and the political/institutional layer is catching up (Trump). Both trends have the same destination: AI agents operating with crypto rails at scale. The infrastructure question is who controls the memory layer when that happens.
