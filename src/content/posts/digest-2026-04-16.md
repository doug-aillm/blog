### 1. Bitcoin Quantum Freeze Proposal Gets Formal (BIP 361)

**What:** A new Bitcoin Improvement Proposal called "Post Quantum Migration and Legacy Signature Sunset" would phase out legacy signature schemes and freeze coins that don't migrate to quantum-resistant addresses. That includes Satoshi's estimated 1.1M BTC in early P2PK outputs.

**Why it matters:** This isn't fringe anymore - it's on Bitcoin's official repository. The proposal would stop users from sending to older address types, then disable the cryptographic methods Bitcoin has used since day one. Migration window would give holders time to move, but unclaimed wallets (including dormant ones) would be frozen.

**The tension:** Immutability is a core Bitcoin principle. Freezing coins - even quantum-vulnerable ones - is a precedent with no clean off-ramp. Expect this debate to run for years.

Source: [CoinDesk Tech](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/04/15/bitcoin-developers-are-trying-to-build-quantum-defenses-your-coins-could-pay-the-price) | [Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/364450/new-bitcoin-proposal-would-freeze-coins-to-counter-quantum-threat)

### 3. AI Models Can't Beat Sports Betting - All 8 Lost Money

**What:** General Reasoning ran 8 frontier models - Claude, Grok, Gemini, GPT-5.4 - through a full Premier League season betting challenge using the Kelly criterion. Every single model lost money. Several went bankrupt.

**Why it matters:** KellyBench is a clean benchmark: it tests whether models can identify genuine edges in a market where the margin is <2%. None could. This matters for the AI trading thesis - financial markets aren't solved by raw reasoning, and the gap between "impressive on benchmarks" and "profitable under adversarial conditions" is real.
