## 📰 Top Stories

### Aave V4 Testnet Deployment Confirmed for Q2
Aave Companies confirmed that V4 testnet will deploy on Sepolia in late April, with mainnet targeted for June pending audits. The new architecture introduces "GHO Liquidity Vaults" that allow users to provide single-sided liquidity for stablecoin pairs while earning GHO yield. Also includes improved liquidation mechanisms using intent-based solvers.

**Why it matters:** Aave's last major upgrade (V3) took 14 months from testnet to mainnet across all chains. V4's modular design should accelerate multi-chain deployment significantly.

### Celestia TIA Staking Participation Hits 73%
Celestia's native token TIA reached 73% staking rate, one of the highest among major L1s. The surge follows the Shwap upgrade (data availability sampling v2) which reduces light node bandwidth requirements by 40%. Over 120 rollups now use Celestia for DA, up from 80 last month.

**Modular thesis:** Data availability is becoming commoditized faster than anyone expected. Celestia's lead in adoption creates strong network effects for rollup developers.

### MakerDAO Raises DSR to 8.5% Following Fed Minutes
MakerDAO governance voted to increase the Dai Savings Rate from 8.0% to 8.5% after Fed meeting minutes suggested rates staying higher for longer. The move keeps DSR competitive with TradFi yields while maintaining the protocol's net interest margin. Total DAI supply increased 2.1% to $5.4B in 24 hours following the announcement.

**DeFi macro:** Real World Assets (RWA) continue driving DeFi yields higher. Maker's exposure to US Treasuries ($2.8B) generates steady revenue even in low-volatility crypto environments.

### Scroll Launches Native Account Abstraction
Scroll zkEVM announced native account abstraction support at the protocol level, making it the first zkRollup to implement ERC-4337 without requiring a separate EntryPoint contract. All accounts on Scroll are now smart accounts by default. Gas sponsorship and batch transactions enabled for all users.

**UX breakthrough:** This eliminates the seed phrase problem for new users. Scroll just made the strongest case yet for why zkRollups > Optimistic rollups for consumer adoption.

### Uniswap DAO Approves Fee Switch Vote Schedule
Uniswap governance passed a proposal to schedule the fee switch vote for March 25-28. The proposal would redirect 10% of trading fees (currently going to LPs) to UNI token holders. Fee distribution would occur monthly via a new staking contract. Analysis shows this would generate ~$400M annual revenue for token holders at current volumes.

**Governance milestone:** After 3+ years of debate, the fee switch is finally happening. This could trigger similar moves across other DeFi protocols.

Skill includes prominent risk warnings and requires users to explicitly acknowledge understanding of options mechanics before enabling trade execution. ## 🌆 Evening Update (3:30 PM GMT+8)

Asian session brought macro turbulence and regulatory clarity. Oil shock, security breaches, and institutional moves dominated headlines. Here's what happened while you were heads-down:

### Bitcoin Dips Below $69.5K as Oil Tanker Attacks Send Brent Above $100
Two oil tankers attacked in Iraqi waters sent Brent crude surging 10%, hitting levels not seen since 2022. Bitcoin slipped to $69,400 as risk-off sentiment swept Asia-Pacific markets (MSCI down 1.8%). Correlation between BTC and equities strengthened during the selloff, but crypto showed relative resilience vs tech stocks which dropped 2.3%.

**Macro watch:** Oil above $100 reignites inflation fears just before tomorrow's U.S. CPI print. If inflation runs hot, Fed rate cut expectations (already slim) evaporate completely. BTC historically performs poorly in stagflation scenarios.

### SEC & CFTC Sign Historic MoU for Combined Crypto Oversight
The two agencies ended years of turf wars with a memorandum of understanding to coordinate crypto regulation. Combined oversight means clearer jurisdictional boundaries - securities tokens go to SEC, commodity tokens and derivatives to CFTC. Industry lawyers expect this to accelerate ETF approvals and reduce regulatory uncertainty.

**Policy shift:** This is the regulatory clarity crypto has been begging for since 2017. Coordinated enforcement should reduce the "regulation by enforcement" chaos that's plagued DeFi protocols.

### Across Protocol's ACX Token Rockets 80% on Plan to Exit DAO Structure
Across (bridging protocol) announced plans to convert from DAO to traditional C-corp, offering ACX holders either equity in the new entity or a 25% premium buyout. This marks one of the first major reversals from decentralized governance to corporate structure. Token surged from $0.32 to $0.58 on the news.

**DAO reality check:** Turns out legal liability, operational efficiency, and investor protection matter. Expect more protocols to follow - especially those with institutional ambitions or regulatory pressure. The DAO experiment isn't dead, but it's evolving.

### Bullish Exchange Overtakes Coinbase as 3rd Largest by Spot Volume
Institutional-focused exchange Bullish saw spot trading jump 62% to $76 billion in February, surpassing Coinbase's market share. The surge follows Bullish's expansion into Asian markets and competitive maker fee rebates. Binance still dominates with 45% global spot volume, followed by OKX at 18%.

**Exchange wars:** Coinbase's retail focus is losing ground to institutional platforms in this cycle. Bullish's backing by Block.one (EOS) and partnerships with traditional finance are paying off.

### European Central Bank Unveils "Appia" Tokenized Finance Roadmap
ECB released comprehensive plan for euro-based tokenized financial system, aiming to reduce EU's reliance on U.S. dollar infrastructure. The Appia roadmap includes wholesale CBDC for settlement, tokenized government bonds, and interoperable DeFi rails. Pilot programs start Q3 2026 with Deutsche Bank, BNP Paribas, and Santander.

**Geopolitical DeFi:** Europe is serious about financial sovereignty. If successful, Appia could challenge dollar dominance in cross-border settlement. This isn't just crypto adoption - it's systemic infrastructure change.

### Crypto Developer Activity Down 75% as Talent Shifts to AI
Code commits across major blockchain networks (Ethereum, Solana, Polkadot, Cosmos) fell 75% year-over-year as developers migrate to AI projects. GitHub activity shows 40% of former crypto contributors now work on LLM infrastructure, agent frameworks, and AI-native apps. Only Bitcoin and privacy-focused chains maintained stable contributor counts.

**Builder exodus:** The narrative shifted from "crypto will change everything" to "AI will change everything." Crypto needs a new value prop beyond speculation to bring builders back. Intersection of crypto + AI (verifiable compute, decentralized training) might be the answer.

### Wells Fargo Files Trademark for "WFUSD" Stablecoin
Wells Fargo submitted trademark application for WFUSD, signaling plans to enter tokenized deposits space. The move mirrors JPMorgan's trademark filing that preceded their deposit token launch on Base (Ethereum L2). Filing covers digital currency services, blockchain-based payment systems, and tokenized assets.

**TradFi adoption:** When America's 4th largest bank files for a stablecoin trademark, it's not an experiment - it's strategic positioning. Expect other major banks to follow once regulatory framework solidifies under SEC-CFTC coordination.

### Bonk.fun Domain Hijacked in Social Engineering Attack
Solana meme coin launchpad Bonk.fun suffered domain hijacking with crypto drainer planted on the site. Users who signed a fake "terms of service" message had wallets drained. Operator "Tom" confirmed only post-breach interactions were affected. Estimated losses: $380K across 40+ wallets. Domain control restored within 3 hours.

**Security reminder:** Domain security is the weakest link in crypto UX. Even legitimate projects with good smart contract security can be compromised via DNS hijacking. Always verify contract addresses independently before signing anything.

### Ethereum Foundation Experiments with DVT-Lite Technology
EF announced testing of Distributed Validator Technology lite version, aimed at making validator operations more accessible without full DVT complexity. DVT-lite allows 2-of-3 key sharing (vs 4-of-7 in full DVT) for solo stakers who want backup key holders. Reduces single-point-of-failure risk while maintaining simpler setup than full DVT.

**Staking UX:** Solo staking remains under 10% of all ETH staked due to technical complexity and key management fear. DVT-lite could help, but Lido's 30% dominance shows most users prefer liquid staking despite centralization concerns.

### Ripple Launches $750M Share Buyback at $50B Valuation
Ripple commenced buyback program for employees and early investors at $50B valuation, up 25% from November's $40B funding round. Offer runs through April. The timing follows XRP's price stabilization near $1.38 and increased regulatory clarity around crypto securities.

**Valuation reality check:** $50B seems rich for a company whose primary product (XRP for cross-border payments) faces competition from SWIFT improvements and CBDC pilots. But Ripple's diversification into custody, stablecoins, and enterprise blockchain may justify premium.
