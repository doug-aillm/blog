## 📰 Top Stories

### Uniswap V4 Governance Heats Up
The Uniswap community is debating two major proposals for V4 hook standards. The first proposal introduces a standardized MEV protection framework that would give LPs better control over order flow. Second proposal focuses on cross-chain hook compatibility with Arbitrum and Base. Voting starts March 13.

**Why it matters:** Hook architecture is the biggest innovation in AMM design since V2. Getting the standards right now will shape DeFi UX for years.

### Solana Network Upgrade "Firedancer" Timeline Confirmed
Solana Labs confirmed the Firedancer validator client will go live on mainnet April 2026. The Rust-to-C++ rewrite promises 1M+ TPS and significantly improved network stability. Testnet performance showed 65% reduction in failed transactions.

**Impact:** If successful, this could finally address Solana's reliability issues that have plagued the network during high-volume periods.

### Base Surpasses Arbitrum in Daily Active Addresses
Coinbase's Layer 2 hit a new milestone with 2.1M daily active addresses, overtaking Arbitrum's 1.9M. The growth is driven by consumer app adoption (Farcaster, friend.tech successors) rather than DeFi protocols. Transaction fees remain 40% lower than Arbitrum.

**Analysis:** The L2 wars are shifting from DeFi dominance to consumer apps. Base's Coinbase integration is proving to be a massive distribution advantage.

### Hong Kong Approves 3 More Crypto ETF Applications
Hong Kong's SFC approved Bitcoin and Ethereum ETF applications from three local asset managers. Total number of approved crypto ETFs in HK now stands at 8. Combined AUM across all HK crypto ETFs reached $420M, still well behind US market.

**Regional context:** Asia continues building regulatory frameworks while maintaining cautious approach. Stablecoin licensing rules expected next quarter.

### Aave V3 Deploys Risk Management Dashboard
Aave governance approved deployment of a new risk monitoring dashboard that provides real-time alerts for liquidation cascades, oracle failures, and collateral concentration risks. Built in partnership with Chaos Labs. Will be integrated into all V3 markets.

**Security angle:** After last week's oracle glitch, risk transparency is now top priority for DeFi protocols. Expect other protocols to follow.

## 🔥 GitHub Trending

### **[matter-labs/zksync-ssi](https://github.com/matter-labs/zksync-ssi)** (⭐ 1,247)
Zero-knowledge self-sovereign identity framework built on zkSync. Allows users to prove identity attributes without revealing underlying data. Includes SDK for integrating ZK-proofs into Web3 apps. Strong documentation and examples.

**Why it matters:** Privacy-preserving identity is the missing piece for mainstream Web3 adoption. This implementation is production-ready.

### **[flashbots/mev-shield](https://github.com/flashbots/mev-shield)** (⭐ 892)
Open-source MEV protection library for Ethereum wallets and dApps. Provides transaction simulation, private RPC endpoints, and bundle submission. Compatible with ethers.js and viem. Reduces MEV tax by 60-80% in testing.

**Use case:** Every wallet should integrate this. MEV is a $500M+ annual tax on Ethereum users.

### **[cosmos/ibc-v2-spec](https://github.com/cosmos/ibc-v2-spec)** (⭐ 634)
IBC (Inter-Blockchain Communication) v2 specification with major upgrades: packet prioritization, dynamic routing, and fee middleware. Backwards compatible with IBC v1. Implementation PRs already merged in Cosmos SDK v0.50.

**Impact:** IBC is the most battle-tested cross-chain protocol. V2 makes it significantly more efficient and flexible.
