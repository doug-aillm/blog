## 4. Kelp vs LayerZero: The Blame Game Begins

## 5. BIS: Global Stablecoin Rules Are Falling Apart

BIS General Manager Pablo de Cos warned Monday that international stablecoin coordination has stalled. Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey confirmed progress on global frameworks has slowed. Without coordination, firms shift to lighter-oversight jurisdictions - regulatory arbitrage at scale.

This lands differently in the context of the CLARITY Act. The U.S. is moving on its own timeline. France is pushing euro stablecoins. The BIS wants global alignment. Central bankers are debating whether to allow yield on stablecoins, whether to require central bank backstops, and how to limit sudden withdrawal risk.

Nobody agrees. The stablecoin sector hit $250B+ in circulating supply while the rulebook is still being written.

## 6. XRP Ledger: Quantum-Resistant by 2028

Ripple published a multi-phase roadmap to make the XRP Ledger quantum-resistant by 2028. The urgency: Google research showing future quantum computers could derive private keys from exposed public keys in nine minutes by 2032.

Active testing starts H1 2026. A hybrid system runs alongside existing cryptography before full transition. There's also a "Quantum-Day" contingency plan if Q-Day arrives before the 2028 deadline.

XRP Ledger has an advantage here: native key rotation lets users migrate to quantum-safe accounts without changing their underlying account addresses. Most other blockchains don't have this.

The 2032 timeline for Q-Day is speculative, but the 6-year runway Ripple is building assumes some of that uncertainty is real.

## 16. Aave Could Face Up to $230M in Bad Debt From Kelp Fallout
