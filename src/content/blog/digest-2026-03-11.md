## X Money launches April with 6% yield as Congress debates stablecoin rules

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/11/dogecoin-zooms-as-elon-musk-announces-x-money-launch-date-for-april)

Elon announced X Money will go live next month with peer-to-peer transfers, bank linking, and a debit card—plus 6% APY on balances. The timing creates an awkward comparison: a fiat fintech product inside a social app gets to offer yields that the CLARITY Act is trying to legislate out of stablecoin products.

## Aave sees $27M in liquidations after wstETH oracle miscalculation

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/03/10/defi-lending-platform-aave-sees-a-rare-usd27-million-liquidations-after-a-price-glitch)

A stale timestamp in Aave's CAPO risk oracle caused it to value wstETH roughly 2.85% below market, triggering $27M in unnecessary liquidations. The protocol incurred no bad debt, but liquidators pocketed 499 ETH in bonuses from what was essentially a math error in the smart contract's exchange rate logic.

## Coinbase-backed x402 protocol processes $28k daily despite $7B ecosystem valuation

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/11/coinbase-backed-ai-payments-protocol-wants-to-fix-micropayment-but-demand-is-just-not-there-yet)

The x402 protocol aims to enable AI agents to pay each other via embedded stablecoin micropayments, but onchain data shows only $28k in daily volume with roughly half flagged as "gamed" transactions. The narrative around agentic commerce is outpacing actual merchant adoption—the pay-per-use APIs and agent-to-agent services that x402 is designed for don't exist at scale yet.

## Cloudflare ships /crawl endpoint for entire websites via single API call

[Cloudflare Changelog](https://developers.cloudflare.com/changelog/post/2026-03-10-br-crawl-endpoint/)

Browser Rendering's new /crawl endpoint lets you submit a starting URL and get back every discovered page as HTML, Markdown, or structured JSON. It handles sitemaps, respects robots.txt, supports incremental crawling via modifiedSince filters, and runs asynchronously—submit a job, get an ID, check back as pages are processed.

## AutoKernel: Give it a PyTorch model, wake up to optimized Triton kernels

[GitHub](https://github.com/RightNow-AI/autokernel)

Inspired by Karpathy's autoresearch, AutoKernel profiles your model's GPU bottlenecks, extracts them as Triton kernels, and runs an autonomous agent overnight to optimize each one. The agent modifies kernel.py, benchmarks with 5-stage correctness checks, and either keeps or reverts changes—about 40 experiments per hour, orchestrated by Amdahl's law to prioritize impact.
