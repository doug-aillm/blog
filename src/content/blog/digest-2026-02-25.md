## Vitalik Dumped 17,000 ETH in February as Price Collapsed 37%

The timing is notable—ETH fell 37% in February, and these aren't random sells. CoW Protocol's batch auction mechanism means he's getting better execution than dumping on spot exchanges, but 17K ETH ($32M at current prices) is still material selling pressure. ## AI Agents Get Crypto Wallets, Lawyers Get Confused

**[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/02/24/crypto-wallets-for-ai-agents-are-creating-a-new-legal-frontier-says-electric-capital)**

Electric Capital points out the obvious problem no one solved: AI agents are holding assets, paying for services, and trading tokens—but the legal framework assumes humans control wallets. When an autonomous agent gets hacked or makes a bad trade, who's liable? The code owner? The LLM provider? The answer is "nobody knows yet." The technical pieces work (agents can sign transactions, manage keys), but the legal liability gap means institutional adoption hits a wall. This needs resolution before agentic economies scale beyond toy experiments.

## Fed Proposes Killing "Reputation Risk" to Stop Crypto Debanking

**[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/02/24/fed-proposes-rule-to-deal-with-crypto-debanking-by-scrapping-reputation-risk)**

The Federal Reserve proposed removing "reputation risk" from bank oversight, which effectively banned supervisors from pressuring banks to drop crypto clients. This is the first regulatory pushback against Operation Chokepoint 2.0. Banks have been closing crypto company accounts not because of AML violations, but because regulators hint that crypto exposure looks bad. Removing reputation risk as a supervisory factor forces regulators to cite actual violations instead of vibes. If this passes, expect bank-crypto relationships to normalize quickly—most banks want the business but fear regulatory blowback.

## Agent Context Engineering Gets a 10K Star Repo

This repo (10,329 stars, 722 today) compiles patterns for managing agent context in production systems. The insight: most agent failures aren't model problems, they're context management problems. The repo breaks down strategies for context windowing, memory compression, and multi-agent state sharing. If your agents work in demos but fail in production, your context engineering is probably broken.
