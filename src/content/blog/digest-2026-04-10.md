## France Launches Government Linux Desktop Plan as Windows Exit Begins

[France's DINUM announces migration from Windows to Linux](https://www.numerique.gouv.fr/sinformer/espace-presse/souverainete-numerique-reduction-dependances-extra-europeennes/) across government systems, with the national health insurance agency already moving 80,000 agents to open-source tools. The directive hits harder than typical tech sovereignty posturing because it comes with actual timelines and ministerial-level accountability. Each French ministry must formalize migration plans by autumn 2026 covering workstations, collaboration tools, antivirus, AI, databases, virtualization, and network equipment.

## Instant 1.0 – A Backend for AI-Coded Apps Raises $17M

[InstantDB shipped 1.0](https://www.instantdb.com/essays/architecture) after 4 years building what they call "the best backend for AI-coded apps." The architecture is clever: multi-tenant Postgres design means spinning up new projects costs kilobytes of RAM instead of hundreds of megabytes for VMs, so apps never freeze and you can create unlimited instances. Their sync engine gives every app multiplayer, offline mode, and optimistic updates by default—the kind of infrastructure Linear, Notion, and Figma had to build custom.

## ETH Zurich Demonstrates 17,000 Qubit Array with 99.91% Fidelity

[ETH Zurich achieved 99.91% precision](https://ethz.ch/en/news-and-events/eth-news/news/2026/04/a-new-trick-brings-stability-to-quantum-operations.html) on swap gates using geometric phases instead of tunnel effects, making the quantum operations robust against laser intensity fluctuations. They pulled off simultaneous gates on 17,000 qubit pairs using cold potassium atoms in optical lattices. The geometric phase approach matters because dynamical phases (tunneling/collisions) degrade with tiny laser imperfections—geometric phases don't.

## Japan Moves to Classify Cryptocurrencies as Financial Products

[Japan's new crypto rules](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/04/10/japan-moves-to-classify-cryptocurrencies-as-financial-products) ban insider trading, require annual issuer disclosures, and impose 10-year prison terms plus ¥10M fines for operating unregistered. The classification as financial products puts crypto under the same regulatory framework as stocks and bonds. This follows Hong Kong awarding its first stablecoin licenses to HSBC and Standard Chartered-led groups under the Stablecoins Ordinance.

## Quantum-Safe Bitcoin Now Possible Without Soft Fork

[StarkWare researcher developed a scheme](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/04/10/quantum-safe-bitcoin-now-possible-without-a-soft-fork-but-costs-usd200-a-pop) that works under existing consensus rules, offering quantum-safe transactions at $200 per transaction. It's an emergency fallback while BIP-360 awaits activation—expensive but functional. XRP may have lower exposure to quantum threats than Bitcoin because fewer addresses leave public keys exposed, though the research is still early.

## Microsoft Suspends Dev Accounts for High-Profile Open Source Projects

[Microsoft suspended developer accounts](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-suspends-dev-accounts-for-high-profile-open-source-projects/) for several major open source projects without warning or clear explanation. The suspensions hit active repositories with thousands of stars and contributors. Microsoft's automated abuse detection systems appear to be flagging legitimate open source activity, with no clear appeals process or human review before action.
