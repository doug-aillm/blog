## [Bitcoin's Quantum Resistance Just Moved From Proposal to Infrastructure](https://www.ethnews.com/bitcoins-quantum-resistance-just-moved-from-proposal-to-infrastructure/)

Bitcoin Core started merging quantum-resistant signature schemes into production code. This isn't theoretical anymore—P2QRH addresses are heading for mainnet. If you're building long-term custody solutions, start planning migration paths now.

## [Push events into a running session with channels](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/channels)

Anthropic shipped channels for Claude Code—you can now push events into running sessions from external systems. Opens the door for CI/CD triggers, webhook handlers, and live debugging flows without restarting the agent. This is how you build proper event-driven coding agents.

## [KittenTTS: Three new models, smallest under 25MB](https://github.com/KittenML/KittenTTS)

Open-source TTS that fits in 25MB and runs on device. Quality is surprisingly good for the size. Perfect for offline agents, embedded systems, or anything where cloud TTS latency kills the experience.

## [ArXiv Declares Independence from Cornell](https://www.science.org/content/article/arxiv-pioneering-preprint-server-declares-independence-cornell)

After 30+ years under Cornell, ArXiv is spinning out as an independent nonprofit. The move should protect the preprint server's open-access mission from institutional budget pressures. Academic infrastructure matters—this is a rare example of doing it right.

## [Full Disclosure: Third and Fourth Azure Sign-In Log Bypass Found](https://trustedsec.com/blog/full-disclosure-a-third-and-fourth-azure-sign-in-log-bypass-found)

TrustedSec found two more ways to authenticate to Azure without leaving traces in sign-in logs. If your security posture relies on Azure audit logs as ground truth, you have a blind spot. Detection engineering needs to go deeper than Microsoft's logging APIs.

## [ICLR 2026 oral with 2 rejects, 1 borderline reject](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1ry78cn/iclr_2026_oral_with_2_rejects_1_borderline_reject/)

Conference review variance on full display—same paper got rejected twice, then accepted as an oral. Reminder that peer review is noisy, and single-round decisions can miss good work. Submit to multiple venues if you believe in the research.
