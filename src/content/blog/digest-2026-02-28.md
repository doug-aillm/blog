## Vitalik Buterin reveals his bold new plan to fix Ethereum's scaling problem

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/02/27/vitalik-buterin-reveals-his-bold-new-plan-to-fix-ethereum-s-scaling-problem)

After years of focusing L2 rollups, Vitalik's now shifting back to scaling Ethereum's base layer directly. This signals either a recognition that the L2-first strategy hit limits, or that new tech finally makes L1 scaling practical—either way, it's a major strategic pivot for the ecosystem's roadmap.

## AI tool catches critical XRP Ledger bug that could have drained wallets

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/02/27/ai-tool-catches-critical-xrp-ledger-bug-that-could-have-drained-wallets)

The vulnerability in the Batch amendment's signature validation was caught during voting and never hit mainnet, but the exploit path was severe enough that validators immediately voted it down. This is one of the first high-profile cases where AI-assisted auditing caught what human reviewers might have missed.

## Unsloth Dynamic 2.0 GGUFs

[Unsloth AI](https://unsloth.ai/docs/basics/unsloth-dynamic-2.0-ggufs)

Unsloth just shipped a new GGUF format that dynamically optimizes quantization per layer instead of applying uniform compression. For anyone running local LLMs, this means better quality-per-gigabyte ratios without manual tuning—think of it as smart compression that adapts to what each layer actually needs.

## A better streams API is possible for JavaScript

[Cloudflare Blog](https://blog.cloudflare.com/a-better-web-streams-api/)

Cloudflare's proposing a cleaner alternative to the current Web Streams API, which has been criticized for being overcomplicated since day one. Their version cuts the boilerplate and makes backpressure handling less error-prone—if it gains traction, this could actually fix one of the web platform's longstanding pain points.

## Polymarket bettors appear to have insider-traded on a market designed to catch insider traders

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/02/27/polymarket-bettors-appear-to-have-insider-traded-on-a-market-designed-to-catch-insider-traders)

At least 12 wallets collectively made over $1 million betting on ZachXBT's investigation outcome before the findings went public, with one trader turning $0.14 entry into $411k. The irony: the market was specifically set up to detect insider trading, and it worked—just not the way anyone expected.
