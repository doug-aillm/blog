## Axios compromised on NPM – malicious versions drop remote access trojan

[Read on StepSecurity](https://www.stepsecurity.io/blog/axios-compromised-on-npm-malicious-versions-drop-remote-access-trojan)

The most-downloaded HTTP client on npm just got hijacked. Attackers published malicious versions of Axios that install a remote access trojan on any machine that pulls the package. If your CI/CD auto-updates dependencies, you might already be running backdoored code. Pin your versions and audit your lockfiles.

## Ollama now runs on MLX for Apple Silicon

[Read on Ollama Blog](https://ollama.com/blog/mlx)

Ollama ditched CPU inference and switched to Apple's MLX framework for M-series chips. Translation: faster local models without burning through your battery. If you've been running Llama or Mistral on a Mac and wondering why it felt slow, this is the fix. MLX turns the Neural Engine into actual usable compute instead of just marketing copy.

## David Bailey's Nakamoto dumps 5% of its Bitcoin treasury

[Read on CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/31/david-bailey-s-nakamoto-sells-roughly-5-of-its-bitcoin-holdings-offloading-284-btc)

Nakamoto Holdings just sold 284 BTC from its treasury—roughly 5% of the stash they've been stacking since pivoting to a "Bitcoin treasury strategy." The stated reason is liquidity pressures, which is corporate speak for "we need cash and can't wait for the next bull run." When treasury companies start selling instead of buying, it's worth noticing.

## Bitcoin demand collapses as real interest rates spike

[Read on CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/31/bitcoin-demand-falters-as-real-interest-rates-surge)

U.S. 10-year TIPS yields are climbing, and Bitcoin is bleeding. Real yields (inflation-adjusted returns on bonds) are back above 2%, which makes zero-yield assets like BTC a tougher sell. When you can lock in guaranteed inflation-beating returns with Treasuries, speculative bets lose their appeal. This is the macro headwind nobody wants to talk about.

## Universal Claude.md – cut Claude output tokens

Someone figured out how to make Claude shut up faster. This repo contains a universal system prompt that trains Claude to stop padding responses with filler, apologies, and redundant confirmations. Drop it into your context and watch token usage drop by 30-40%. If you're paying per-token or hitting rate limits, this is the efficiency hack you didn't know you needed.

## Hashdex launches options on its diversified crypto ETF

[Read on CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/31/hashdex-s-diversified-crypto-etf-adds-options-for-hedging-income-generation)

Hashdex's NCIQ ETF now supports options trading, which means you can finally hedge a basket of crypto exposure without managing multiple positions. This is the first diversified crypto ETF with options, so it opens up strategies that were only possible with single-asset ETFs like spot Bitcoin. Institutions love optionality—literally.
