## Mistral releases Leanstral: Open-source agent for formal proof engineering

[Mistral AI](https://mistral.ai/news/leanstral)

Mistral just dropped Leanstral, a 6B-parameter agent that writes code and formally proves its correctness in Lean 4. The pitch is straightforward: instead of humans spending hours verifying machine-generated code, the agent ships with mathematical proofs that it works. At pass@2, it costs $36 and beats Claude Sonnet (which runs $549) by 2.6 points on their FLTEval benchmark—testing against real PRs from the Fermat's Last Theorem formalization project, not competition math. Apache 2.0 licensed, free API endpoint during the feedback period.

## Ethereum Foundation's new mandate divides the community

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/03/16/ethereum-foundation-s-new-mandate-sparks-debate-about-its-role-priorities)

The 38-page constitutional document landed Friday, and reactions split immediately. Supporters see it as overdue clarity on the Foundation's role as neutral steward. Critics argue it's doubling down on philosophy when Ethereum needs aggressive business development—especially as institutions finally move onchain. Dankrad Feist pointed out that almost nobody on core dev calls cares about real-world usage patterns, and Coinbase's Yuga Cohler compared it to Netscape rewriting their browser while Microsoft ate their lunch. The timing matters: this hits right as the network faces leadership transitions and debates over whether to scale L1 directly or keep betting on L2 rollups.

## Visa and Coinbase are building competing payment layers for AI agents

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/03/15/visa-is-ready-for-ai-agents-so-is-coinbase-they-re-building-very-different-internets)

When your AI queries six APIs to research an article, those micropayments ($0.002 per call) cost more to process through Stripe ($0.30 minimum) than the actual service. That's why Coinbase built x402—embedding stablecoin payments directly into HTTP requests so agents pay and continue in one interaction. Cloudflare, Circle, AWS, and Stripe back it; Google's agent payment standard includes it as settlement. Current volume is $28k/day with half flagged as artificial, but Brian Armstrong predicts agents will outnumber humans in transactions, and CZ went further: one million times more payments than people, all in crypto. Meanwhile Visa shipped Trusted Agent Protocol last October, and Mastercard just completed Europe's first live AI-agent bank payment inside Santander—both on existing card rails.

## Meta renews commitment to jemalloc after community backlash

[Meta Engineering](https://engineering.fb.com/2026/03/02/data-infrastructure/investing-in-infrastructure-metas-renewed-commitment-to-jemalloc/)

Meta admitted they strayed from core engineering principles with jemalloc—the high-performance memory allocator that's been foundational to their infrastructure. After community feedback (and conversations with founder Jason Evans), they unarchived the original repo and committed to paying down technical debt instead of chasing short-term wins. The new roadmap focuses on hugepage allocator improvements, better memory efficiency, and adapting to emerging hardware. This is rare: a major tech company publicly acknowledging they took on too much technical debt in critical infrastructure and committing to fix it instead of forking or abandoning.

## Bitcoin shorts liquidated in $485M squeeze as price tests $75k

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/393850/bitcoin-higher-sentiment-improves)

Bitcoin hit $75,253 (up 3.93%) while Ethereum jumped 8.2% to $2,352. The move triggered $609 million in total liquidations over 24 hours, with $485.6 million coming from short positions. Bitcoin's now on an 8-day winning streak—historically rare, though the 2022 bear market had one too. What's notable: equity, oil, and bond markets have been volatile, but bitcoin traders haven't panicked. Sentiment flipped while traditional markets stayed jittery.
