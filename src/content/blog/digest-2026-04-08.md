## AI & Dev

**[LLMs May Be Standardizing Human Expression](https://dornsife.usc.edu/news/stories/ai-may-be-making-us-think-and-write-more-alike/)** — USC researchers argue that billions using the same handful of chatbots is shrinking cognitive diversity worldwide. When LLM outputs favor Western educated perspectives and linear reasoning over intuition, entire societies lose problem-solving approaches that might have worked better.

**[We Found an Undocumented Bug in Apollo 11 Code](https://www.juxt.pro/blog/a-bug-on-the-dark-side-of-the-moon/)** — A resource lock in the Apollo Guidance Computer's gyro code leaks when the IMU is caged mid-torque, silently disabling platform realignment. The code was scrutinized by thousands, emulated instruction-by-instruction, and formally verified—but behavioral specification modeling (via Allium) found a missing four-byte cleanup that would have left Michael Collins unable to align for rendezvous behind the Moon.

**[Are We Idiocracy Yet?](https://idiocracy.wtf/)** — Minimal site tracking cultural decay metrics. Sometimes the best commentary is just raw data and a pointed question.

## Crypto & DeFi

**[Ethereum Researchers Propose 'Blobs Over Blocks'](https://www.theblock.co/post/396669/blocks-are-dead-long-live-blobs-ethereum-researchers-explore-upgrade-to-ease-validator-data-burden)** — The upgrade would shift Ethereum validators from storing full blocks to just blob commitments, cutting storage and sync time without sacrificing security. If blob expiry can work for L2s, why can't the L1 run lighter too?

**[NYT Claims Adam Back May Be Satoshi; He Denies It](https://www.theblock.co/post/396663/nyt-investigation-suggests-adam-back-may-be-satoshi-nakamoto)** — New York Times investigation points to Adam Back as a potential Satoshi candidate based on writing style and Hashcash connections. Back dismissed it immediately, but the timing analysis and linguistic forensics are more thorough than previous attempts.

**[South Korea Bringing RWAs and Stablecoins Under Existing Financial Frameworks](https://www.theblock.co/post/396658/south-korea-rwa-stablecoin)** — Rather than inventing new regulation, South Korea's FSC is extending traditional securities and banking rules to cover tokenized assets and fiat-backed stables. Faster than waiting for bespoke crypto law, but means compliance overhead starts day one.

**[Swiss Banks Join Franc Stablecoin Sandbox](https://www.theblock.co/post/396650/swiss-franc-stablecoin-sandbox)** — UBS, Sygnum, and PostFinance are now testing Swiss franc-backed stablecoins in a regulatory sandbox. When traditional banks start issuing tokenized currency, it's no longer an experiment—it's infrastructure pilot season.
