## OpenAI releases GPT-5.4 with native computer-use capabilities

[OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-4/)

GPT-5.4 is the first general-purpose model from OpenAI with state-of-the-art computer-use built in, letting agents operate across applications and carry out complex multi-step workflows. It also handles 1M token context windows and introduces "tool search" to help agents find the right tools without manual orchestration. The token efficiency improvements mean GPT-5.4 solves problems using significantly fewer tokens than GPT-5.2, which translates to faster speeds and lower costs for production deployments.

## Vancouver city staff recommend dropping Bitcoin reserve motion

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/392521/vancouver-drop-btc-reserve)

Vancouver's city staff have asked the council to drop a motion that would establish a Bitcoin reserve, arguing that Bitcoin is not an "allowable asset" under municipal investment policies. The recommendation highlights the regulatory constraints facing local governments that want to hold crypto assets, even as some U.S. municipalities have moved forward with similar proposals. Canada's municipal finance rules appear to draw a harder line than their American counterparts.

## Fed clarifies that tokenized securities follow existing capital rules

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/392506/fed-clarifies-rules-tokenized-securities-framework-technology-neutral)

The Federal Reserve issued guidance stating that tokenized securities are subject to the same capital treatment as traditional securities, describing its framework as "technology neutral." Banks don't need special capital reserves just because an asset is on-chain. This removes a layer of regulatory uncertainty for financial institutions exploring tokenization, though it also means tokenized assets don't get preferential treatment either.

## OKX launches in-app social trading network

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/392524/okx-launches-social-trading-network)

OKX added a social networking feature that lets traders share strategies, follow other users, and see real-time activity feeds—an attempt to reduce the "trust gap" in crypto trading by surfacing what experienced traders are actually doing. The move puts OKX in competition with standalone platforms like eToro and Wealthfront's social features. Whether traders want social feeds embedded in their exchange or prefer keeping those functions separate is the open question.

## "We Might All Be AI Engineers Now"

[Yas](https://yasint.dev/we-might-all-be-ai-engineers-now/)

A developer writes about the shift from writing code by hand to designing systems that AI agents execute. The core argument: the skill isn't writing boilerplate anymore—it's knowing what to build, how to architect it, and when the model is wrong. Without foundational knowledge, AI-generated code turns into unmaintainable spaghetti; with it, you ship structured software in hours instead of days. The post doubles as a hiring filter: teams that aren't paying attention to this shift are the ones to avoid.
