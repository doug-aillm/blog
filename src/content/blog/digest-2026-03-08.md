## Kalshi and Polymarket each exploring $20B valuations, double their previous rounds

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/392755/kalshi-and-polymarket-each-exploring-fundraising-at-20-billion-valuations-double-previous-rounds-wsj)

Both platforms are in talks for new funding at valuations 2x their last rounds. Kalshi's regulated approach and Polymarket's crypto-native volume both proved product-market fit during the 2024-2025 election cycle—now investors are pricing in prediction markets as a permanent alternative to traditional polling and sentiment tracking.

## First US state-level stablecoin bill passes in Florida

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/392733/first-us-state-level-stablecoin-bill-passes-in-florida)

Florida's Senate approved a framework allowing state-regulated stablecoin issuance before federal clarity arrives. This sets a precedent for state-level crypto regulation and could accelerate adoption if other states follow—though interoperability with future federal rules remains uncertain.

## SWE-CI: Evaluating Agent Capabilities in Maintaining Codebases via CI

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03823)

New benchmark measures how well AI agents can maintain codebases by fixing CI failures. Unlike code generation benchmarks, this tests whether agents can navigate real repos, diagnose test failures, and apply fixes that don't break other tests—closer to what junior engineers actually do.

## Autoresearch: Agents researching on single-GPU nanochat training automatically

[GitHub/Karpathy](https://github.com/karpathy/autoresearch)

Karpathy's new framework lets AI agents propose experiments, run training loops on a single GPU, analyze results, and iterate. Think of it as a research assistant that doesn't need supervision between runs—early but promising signal for automated ML research at smaller scales.

## Cloud VM benchmarks 2026

[Ecuadors](https://devblog.ecuadors.net/cloud-vm-benchmarks-2026-performance-price-1i1m.html)

Comprehensive performance-per-dollar comparison across AWS, GCP, Azure, and smaller providers. Google's c4 instances and Hetzner's ARM offerings lead on price-performance, while AWS continues to charge premium for convenience. Worth checking before spinning up compute-heavy workloads.

## Coinbase Prime rolls out unified cross margin across spot, derivatives and regulated perps

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/392716/coinbase-prime-unified-cross-margin-spot-derivatives-regulated-perps)

Prime clients can now collateralize spot, derivatives, and perpetual futures positions under one margin pool. This matches what offshore exchanges have offered for years but with US regulatory compliance—lowers capital requirements for institutional traders who previously had to silo risk across product lines.
