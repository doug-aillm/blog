## Drift's $280M exploit—admin takeover, not a contract bug

[Read on The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/396183/drift-280m-exploit-zachxbt-circle)

The Solana perps platform Drift got hit for $280 million through what they're calling a "sophisticated admin takeover," not a smart contract vulnerability. ZachXBT's already criticizing Circle's USDC handling in the aftermath, which suggests frozen addresses and messy recovery ahead. This marks another case where the weakest link wasn't the code but whoever had the keys.

## Email obfuscation: what actually stops spammers in 2026

[Read on spencermortensen.com](https://spencermortensen.com/articles/email-obfuscation/)

Someone tested 15 obfuscation techniques against 318 real spambots. HTML entities still block 95% of harvesters despite being trivial to decode, which says more about bot quality than technique effectiveness. JavaScript-based protection (concatenation, rot18, AES) blocks 100% but breaks accessibility. The real takeaway: most harvesters are lazier than you'd think, and combining multiple weak techniques beats one "strong" one.

## GLM-5V-Turbo: vision-to-code for GUI automation

[Read on Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/products/z-ai)

New foundation model from Zhipu AI that converts screenshots into working automation code. Point it at a GUI, it writes Playwright scripts to interact with it—no CSS selectors, no manual element inspection. The use case is obvious for anyone who's written brittle UI tests or scrapers. If it holds up against dynamic SPAs, this could replace a lot of tedious DOM spelunking.

## IBM + Arm: betting on custom silicon for enterprise

[Read on IBM Newsroom](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2026-04-02-ibm-announces-strategic-collaboration-with-arm-to-shape-the-future-of-enterprise-computing)

IBM's partnering with Arm to build custom silicon for enterprise workloads. Translation: they're designing chips optimized for their cloud stack instead of buying off-the-shelf x86. This is the same playbook AWS ran with Graviton—own the full vertical, squeeze margins, lock customers into proprietary performance. If you're building on IBM Cloud, expect Arm-native instances to become the default in 12-18 months.

## Metaplanet adds 5,075 BTC, now third-largest public holder

[Read on The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/396190/metaplanet-adds-5075-bitcoin-bringing-total-holdings-to-40177-btc-to-become-third-largest-among-public-companies)

Japanese holding company Metaplanet just added 5,075 bitcoin, bringing their total to 40,177 BTC. That makes them the third-largest public company holder after MicroStrategy and Marathon. They're executing the same treasury strategy Saylor pioneered—buying bitcoin with debt at low rates and betting the appreciation outpaces interest. Market's down 3-5% today, which likely means they got a discount on the latest tranche.

## Bonus: SoFi merges fiat + crypto for business banking

[Read on The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/396195/sofi-launches-big-business-banking-combining-fiat-and-crypto-on-a-single-regulated-platform)

SoFi rolled out "Big Business Banking" that unifies fiat and crypto on one regulated platform. Businesses can hold USD and crypto in the same account, switch between them, and settle payments without leaving the app. This is the first real attempt at bridging TradFi and DeFi for SMBs that doesn't feel like duct-taping two separate products together. If the UX doesn't suck, it could force other banks to stop treating crypto like a separate service tier.
