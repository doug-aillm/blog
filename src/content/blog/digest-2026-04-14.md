## Links Worth Your Time

### [Someone bought 30 WordPress plugins and planted a backdoor in all of them](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=42821234)
Supply chain attacks just got personal. An attacker acquired legitimate plugins with established user bases, then pushed malicious updates. This is why dependency audits matter—trust erodes fast when the maintainer changes hands.

### [GitHub Stacked PRs](https://github.blog/changelog/2025-04-14-stacked-pull-requests/)
Finally. GitHub now supports stacked PRs natively, letting you chain dependent changes without third-party tools. If you're used to Gerrit or graphite.dev, this brings that workflow into the mainline. Velocity just got easier.

### [Nothing Ever Happens: Polymarket bot that always buys No](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=42823456)
Someone built a bot that only buys "No" on non-sports prediction markets and it's profitable. Turns out people consistently overestimate the probability of dramatic events. Behavioral economics meets crypto gambling, and the house always loses to the cynic.

### [Introspective Diffusion Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.06789)
New diffusion approach for language models that iteratively refines outputs by "looking inward" at intermediate states. Less hallucination, better coherence, but way slower. Trade compute for accuracy—same pattern we've seen in every AI breakthrough since AlphaGo.

### [Distributed DuckDB Instance](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=42822876)
DuckDB goes multi-node. What started as an embedded analytics database now scales horizontally. If you've been wanting SQLite-level simplicity with Spark-level scale, this might be your answer. Still early, but the architecture is clean.
