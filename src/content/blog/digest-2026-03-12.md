## Temporal: The 9-year journey to fix time in JavaScript

[Bloomberg Engineering](https://bloomberg.github.io/js-blog/post/temporal/)

After nearly a decade in development, JavaScript's Temporal API is finally reaching Stage 3, replacing the notoriously broken Date object with proper timezone handling and calendar support. This fixes one of the language's oldest pain points—managing time zones and DST transitions without pulling in moment.js or date-fns.

## Making WebAssembly a first-class language on the Web

[Mozilla Hacks](https://hacks.mozilla.org/2026/02/making-webassembly-a-first-class-language-on-the-web/)

Mozilla is pushing for WebAssembly to get native devtools integration, source maps that actually work, and direct DOM access without JavaScript glue code. Right now WASM still feels like a compile target bolted onto the web—this proposal would make it a peer to JavaScript in the browser runtime.

## SEC and CFTC commit to work together on crypto policy and introduction of new products

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/393317/sec-cftc-crypto-policy-mou)

The two regulators signed a memorandum of understanding to coordinate on crypto oversight instead of fighting turf wars. This could mean faster product approvals and clearer regulatory boundaries, though skeptics note that past coordination attempts have produced more meetings than actual policy changes.

## Show HN: s@: decentralized social networking over static sites

[satproto.org](http://satproto.org/)

This protocol lets users publish social feeds as static files that peers can subscribe to via RSS-like mechanisms, with replies stored as separate files that reference the original. It's basically rebuilding Twitter on top of web hosting instead of databases—slow, awkward, but genuinely censorship-resistant without requiring blockchain consensus.

## Optimism's OP Labs cuts 20% of staff to 'do fewer things well'

[The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/393339/optimism-op-labs-layoff)

OP Labs is cutting its team from around 80 to 64 people, citing a need to narrow focus after expanding too quickly. The layoffs hit research and ecosystem teams hardest while keeping core protocol development intact—a pattern we're seeing across L2s as the market rewards execution over exploration.

## Binance.US names new CEO as crypto exchange eyes expansion

[Cointelegraph](https://cointelegraph.com/news/binance-us-names-new-ceo-crypto-exchange-eyes-expansion)

Norman Reed takes over as CEO during a quieter regulatory moment, with the exchange planning to add new state licenses and token listings after two years of defensive contraction. The timing suggests Binance.US thinks the worst of its legal exposure is behind it, though federal investigations aren't formally closed.
