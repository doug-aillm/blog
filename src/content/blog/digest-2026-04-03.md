## Google Releases Gemma 4 Open Models

[Gemma 4 announcement](https://deepmind.google/models/gemma/gemma-4/) — Google shipped their latest open-weight models with three size variants (9B, 26B, and a monster 60B). The 26B already runs on Mac mini hardware without melting your desk, which is wild considering where we were 12 months ago.

## Drift Protocol Hit for $280M

[Cointelegraph coverage](https://cointelegraph.com/news/drift-protocol-contact-hacker-onchain-message) — Solana perps protocol Drift lost $280M to an exploit and immediately started sending onchain messages to wallets tied to the attack. Bold move contacting thieves via transaction memos, but negotiations have worked before (see: Poly Network 2021).

## EU Backs Down on Digital Regulation

[HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47624855) — Brussels caved to US pressure and softened its digital regulation stance after threats of trade retaliation. The "digital sovereignty" push lasted about as long as most New Year's resolutions. Companies lobbying against compliance requirements can now breathe easier.

## Aave Avoided Bad Debt by Dumping Risk on Borrowers

[Bank of Canada study](https://cointelegraph.com/news/aave-v3-bad-debt-liquidations-borrower-losses-study) — Research paper found Aave V3 kept its balance sheet clean during 2024's volatility, but the cost got passed to borrowers through aggressive liquidations. Protocol health metrics look pristine when you liquidate users early and often.

## OpenAI Acquires TBPN

[The Verge report](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/906022/openai-buys-tbpn) — OpenAI bought TBPN in what looks like a talent acquisition more than a product play. The startup focused on reasoning models, which tracks with OpenAI's O-series development. Expect those engineers to disappear into the labs for the next year.

## Ollama + Gemma 4 Setup Guide

[Setup gist](https://gist.github.com/greenstevester/fc49b4e60a4fef9effc79066c1033ae5) — Someone documented the complete setup for running Gemma 4 26B locally with Ollama on Apple Silicon. Covers the annoying bits like memory allocation and model quantization. Bookmark if you're tired of API bills.

## Show HN: European App Alternatives Directory

[only-eu.eu](https://only-eu.eu/en/) — Directory of EU-based alternatives to US tech services. Think "Hetzner instead of AWS" energy. Useful if data residency requirements have you scrambling or you just want to avoid US jurisdiction.
