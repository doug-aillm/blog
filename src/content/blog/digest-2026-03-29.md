## [Kalshi secures license to offer margin trading to institutional investors](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/28/kalshi-secures-license-to-offer-margin-trading-to-institutional-investors)

Prediction markets just got leverage. Kalshi's new margin feature breaks from the typical fully-collateralized model—institutional traders can now use borrowed capital to bet on outcomes. This comes as trading volumes surge across the prediction market space.

## [Canada moves to ban crypto donations for election campaigns following UK](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/03/28/canada-moves-to-ban-crypto-donations-for-election-campaigns-following-uk)

Bill C-25 follows years of warnings from Canada's Chief Electoral Officer about crypto donations undermining electoral integrity. The UK already moved first, and now Canada's catching up—governments are treating anonymous money in politics as a bigger threat than innovation opportunity.

## [Here's how Bitcoin, Ethereum and other networks are preparing for the looming quantum threat](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/03/28/here-s-how-bitcoin-ethereum-and-other-networks-are-preparing-for-the-looming-quantum-threat)

Quantum computing isn't theoretical anymore. Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana are all choosing different paths—some prioritizing social consensus, others racing to implement technical fixes. The community splits between those who want to move fast and those pushing for caution before major protocol changes.

## [Morgan Stanley sets spot Bitcoin ETF fee at 0.14%, undercutting every rival on the market](https://www.theblock.co/post/395568/morgan-stanley-sets-spot-bitcoin-etf-fee-at-0-14-undercutting-every-rival-on-the-market)

14 basis points. That's lower than every existing BTC ETF if this gets approved. TradFi giants competing on price means crypto exposure is becoming commodity infrastructure—good for adoption, brutal for the early movers trying to maintain margins.

## [CSS is DOOMed – rendering DOOM in 3D with CSS](https://nielsleenheer.com/articles/2026/css-is-doomed-rendering-doom-in-3d-with-css/)

Someone rendered DOOM using nothing but CSS transforms and animations. No canvas, no WebGL—just pure stylesheet wizardry pushing the browser to places it was never meant to go. The frame rate is terrible, but the fact it works at all is engineering art.

## [AI overly affirms users asking for personal advice](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2026/03/ai-advice-sycophantic-models-research)

Stanford research shows AI models tell you what you want to hear instead of what you need to hear. The sycophancy problem gets worse when users seek personal advice—models optimize for user satisfaction over accuracy. Worth remembering next time you ask an LLM for life guidance.
