## [ARC-AGI-3: Testing AI Through Experience, Not Answers](https://arcprize.org/arc-agi/3)

The new ARC benchmark ditches static puzzles for interactive environments where agents must learn goals on the fly and adapt through experience. Instead of measuring final answers, it tracks how efficiently AI acquires skills over time - measuring the gap between machine and human learning. A 100% score means agents can beat every game as efficiently as humans do. The platform includes replayable runs for inspecting decision timelines and a dev toolkit for integration.

## [Running Tesla Model 3's Computer From Crashed Car Parts](https://bugs.xdavidhu.me/tesla/2026/03/23/running-tesla-model-3s-computer-on-my-desk-using-parts-from-crashed-cars/)

Security researcher bought salvaged Tesla parts off eBay ($200-300 for MCU, $175 for touchscreen) to participate in Tesla's bug bounty. The trick was sourcing the display cable - Tesla publishes full wiring diagrams publicly, but the special Rosenberger connector is impossible to buy retail. Solution: BMW LVDS cables from automotive suppliers turned out close enough. The full setup pulls 8A at 12V peak, making the beefy power supply choice prescient.

## [Irish Drug Dealer's Lost $400M Bitcoin Stack Moves After Years](https://info.arkm.com/research/clifton-collins-irish-drug-dealers-lost-btc-stack-worth-400m-has-woken-up)

Clifton Collins bought 6,000 BTC in 2011-2012, went to jail in 2017, and supposedly lost his keys when his landlord sent his belongings (including a fishing rod containing the seed phrase) to a German incinerator. This week $35M moved to Coinbase Custody. Irish police confirmed seizing "€30M in cryptocurrency" with Europol support. Three theories: police operation, Collins lied about losing keys, or the 2020 burglary at his house grabbed them first.

## [90% of Claude Code Commits Go to Repos With <2 GitHub Stars](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47526036)

Claude's Code tracker shows 114,785 new repos this week and +30.7B lines of net code delta, but the distribution is telling - the vast majority of AI-assisted code ends up in low-visibility projects. Top languages are TypeScript, Python, and JavaScript. Whether this reflects learning projects, internal tools, or quality distribution is unclear, but it maps the real adoption pattern: lots of experimentation, not just the showcase repos.

## [Swift 6.3 Released](https://www.swift.org/blog/swift-6.3-released/)

Latest Swift update ships with improvements to concurrency features, better Windows support, and refinements to the type system. The release continues Swift's push toward full data race safety through compile-time enforcement.
