## AI & Security

**[NSA Uses Mythos Despite Pentagon Blacklist](https://awesomeagents.ai/news/nsa-uses-mythos-despite-pentagon-blacklist/)** — The NSA is running Anthropic's Mythos Preview in production while the Pentagon (its parent department) formally classifies Anthropic as a supply chain risk and fights in court to bar federal agencies from the platform. Mythos was released to ~40 orgs under restricted access; the NSA was one of the unnamed recipients. This lands two days after Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei met with Susie Wiles and Scott Bessent at the White House to discuss how non-Pentagon agencies can access the model. One arm of Defense runs it; another arm sues to stop it.

**[Vercel Breach Traced to Context.ai OAuth Token Theft](https://awesomeagents.ai/news/vercel-context-ai-oauth-supply-chain/)** — Vercel confirmed an April 19 intrusion that pivoted from compromised OAuth tokens at AI office-suite startup Context.ai into a Vercel employee's Google Workspace, then into internal systems holding non-sensitive environment variables. The root cause traces back to June 2025 when Context.ai's consumer AWS environment was compromised; the company only shut it down in March 2026. OAuth tokens stolen nearly a year ago became the wedge. CEO Guillermo Rauch called the attacker "considerably accelerated by AI," though no IOC backs that claim. Another developer platform breached through an AI tool.

**[GitHub's Fake Star Economy](https://awesomeagents.ai/news/openclaw-star-audit/)** — Awesome Agents ran their fake-star methodology against GitHub repos and found massive bot-driven star inflation across the ecosystem. They sampled 361,000 star profiles and fork ratios. Most major repos look clean, but one with 6,532 claimed stars vanished entirely. The audit highlights how stars have become a vanity metric vulnerable to gaming. If you're evaluating tools by GitHub stars, you're trusting a system that doesn't verify identity.

## Dev Tools & 3D

**[TRELLIS.2 Native on Apple Silicon](https://github.com/shivampkumar/TRELLIS-on-Silicon)** — Image-to-3D generation now runs natively on Apple Silicon without PyTorch or CUDA dependencies. TRELLIS.2 converts a single image into a 3D model locally. The port strips out GPU-specific code and optimizes for Metal. This is the pattern: models that required data center GPUs six months ago now run on laptops. Inference keeps getting cheaper and faster.

**[Swiss AI Initiative Launches](https://swiss-ai.org)** — Switzerland formalized its national AI strategy with the Swiss AI Initiative, backed by federal funding and university partnerships. The focus is on trustworthy AI, federated learning, and privacy-preserving ML. Switzerland is positioning itself as the neutral ground for AI development between US hyperscalers and EU regulation. Worth watching if you care about alternative governance models.

**[Zero-Copy Protobuf + ConnectRPC for Rust](https://medium.com/@iainmcgin/zero-copy-protobuf-connectrpc-rust)** — Iain McGinniss shipped a Rust implementation of ConnectRPC that skips serialization overhead by working directly with message buffers. Instead of deserializing into heap-allocated structs, the library reads fields on-demand from the wire format. This cuts allocation and CPU time for high-throughput services. The tradeoff is stricter lifetime management, but for services bottlenecked on RPC overhead, it's worth it.
