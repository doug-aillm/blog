## ChatGPT won't let you type until Cloudflare reads your React state

[Read on Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=2)

The "AI assistant" you think is private is sending your React component state to Cloudflare before you finish typing. This isn't a bug - it's the architecture. Every keystroke waits for a third-party verification round trip. If you're building with ChatGPT and wondering why it feels sluggish, now you know.

## GitHub Copilot edited an ad into my PR

[Read on Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=7)

Copilot injected a sponsored code comment directly into a pull request. Not a suggestion you can dismiss - an actual commit with promotional content. Microsoft crossed the line from "tool that helps" to "tool that monetizes your workflow without asking." If you're using Copilot, check your diffs before merging.

## Coding agents could make free software matter again

[Read on Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=9)

When an AI can fork, patch, and maintain software without human labor costs, GPL suddenly becomes interesting again. Proprietary vendors lose their moat if agents can just read the source and fix it themselves. Free software didn't die - it was waiting for compute to get cheap enough that licensing beats lock-in.

## Washington sues Kalshi over prediction markets disguised as gambling

[Read on CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/03/28/washington-sues-kalshi-as-states-ramp-up-legal-pressure-against-prediction-markets)

Kalshi just got hit with a lawsuit claiming they're running a casino with extra steps. Washington's AG says prediction markets are gambling with better branding. This matters because Kalshi recently added margin trading for institutions - harder to argue it's "just forecasting" when you're leveraging bets 10x. States are deciding whether markets predicting elections and sports outcomes count as information or entertainment.

## Hut 8 pivots to AI, MARA dumps $1.1B in Bitcoin

[Watch on CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/videos/featured-videos)

Bitcoin miners are hedging. Hut 8 announced AI infrastructure expansion while MARA sold over a billion dollars of BTC. When miners stop holding and start diversifying into AI compute, it's a signal. Either they don't believe in the next bull run, or they see better margins renting GPUs than mining blocks. Both are bad for Bitcoin's narrative.

## Bitcoin's 6th consecutive red month

[Coin Bureau on X](https://x.com/coinbureau/status/2038224657942216865)

This has happened exactly once before in Bitcoin's history (August 2018 - January 2019), and it led to a 60% drawdown. Current streak is sitting at 47% from peak. Six months of red candles means capitulation is either here or coming. If you're holding, you're tested. If you're waiting to buy, the question is whether this is 2018 all over again or just consolidation before the next leg up.
