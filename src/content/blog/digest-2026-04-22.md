## SpaceX announces $60 billion Cursor acquisition

[Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/)

Elon Musk's SpaceX disclosed an agreement to acquire Cursor, the AI code editor built on VS Code, for $60 billion—making it one of the largest developer tool acquisitions in history. The move connects Musk's AI infrastructure ambitions directly to developer workflows, suggesting SpaceX sees coding agents as infrastructure rather than product. Expect tight integration with xAI's models and probably a rebrand within six months.

## OpenAI ships ChatGPT Images 2.0 with DALL-E 4 under the hood

[Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/)

OpenAI released ChatGPT Images 2.0, powered by DALL-E 4, with noticeable improvements in photorealism, text rendering, and instruction following. The update handles complex multi-object compositions better and finally stops mangling hands in most cases. What stands out: the images don't scream "AI-generated" anymore—they've crossed into the range where you'd need to look twice.

## Vercel breach exposes OAuth risk in platform environment variables

[Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/)

Attackers exploited Vercel's OAuth flow to gain access to customer environment variables, including API keys and secrets stored in project configs. The breach highlights a structural problem: when platforms store secrets for CI/CD convenience, one compromised OAuth token can leak credentials across hundreds of projects. If you're using Vercel, rotate your secrets and consider moving sensitive keys to external vaults like 1Password or Doppler.

## DeFi loses $295 million in back-to-back exploits

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/)

Kelp DAO lost $292 million in an exploit targeting its rsETH liquid staking token, followed two days later by Volo Protocol losing $3.5 million across WBTC, XAUm, and USDC vaults. Traders on Polymarket are pricing low odds of Kelp socializing losses, which means rsETH holders could be stuck with undercollateralized tokens. These aren't smart contract bugs—they're increasingly sophisticated attacks on DeFi infrastructure that banks are now watching closely enough to pause blockchain plans.

## Strategy drops $2.5 billion on Bitcoin as it tests $78K resistance

[CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/)

MicroStrategy (now Strategy) disclosed its largest Bitcoin purchase in 17 months—$2.5 billion worth—while BTC hovers at the $78,000 resistance level with $180 million in short liquidations at risk. The Coinbase premium has stayed positive for 14 straight days, the longest streak since October's $126K all-time high. Trump's extension of the Iran ceasefire helped the rally, but the real test is whether momentum traders pile in or another Kelp-sized exploit kills the vibe.

## GitHub Copilot individual plans get restructured pricing

[Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/)

GitHub updated Copilot's individual subscription with new pricing tiers and model access. The changes separate casual users from power users, with higher-tier plans unlocking multi-model support (GPT-4.5, Claude) and increased usage limits. The move signals that GitHub sees AI coding assistants as mature enough to segment by intensity of use rather than treating all devs the same. If you're on the old plan, check whether the new tiers save or cost you money.

## Cal.diy: community-forked open-source alternative to Cal.com

[Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/)

After Cal.com moved parts of its codebase behind paid tiers, the community forked the last fully open-source version into Cal.diy. It's now maintained independently with all scheduling features free and self-hostable. This follows a pattern: when open-source companies pull back access, someone always forks and picks up the torch. If you were frustrated by Cal.com's recent licensing changes, Cal.diy is the no-strings version.
