## Bitcoin Mining Difficulty Drops 7.8% as Miners Pivot to AI

[The Block reports](https://www.theblock.co/post/394579/bitcoin-mining-difficulty-drops-7-8-as-miner-exodus-accelerates-amid-ai-pivot) that Bitcoin's mining difficulty just dropped 7.8%, the largest decline in over a year. Miners are switching hardware to AI compute, chasing better margins than they can squeeze from proof-of-work. This is what happens when electricity costs more than block rewards.

## POSSE: The IndieWeb Concept Making a Comeback

[IndieWeb's POSSE documentation](https://indieweb.org/POSSE) (Publish on your Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere) is getting fresh attention. Post to your domain first, then push copies to social silos—you keep canonical URLs, own your content, and don't get rug-pulled when platforms implode. It's not new (coined 2012), but with Twitter collapsing into X and social networks fragmenting, POSSE makes more sense than ever.

## Manyana: Bram Cohen's CRDT-Based Version Control

BitTorrent creator Bram Cohen just dropped [Manyana](https://github.com/bramcohen/manyana), a proof-of-concept for version control built on Conflict-Free Replicated Data Types. Merges never fail, and conflicts show you what each side actually did instead of dumping opaque blobs. The demo is 470 lines of Python; the idea is that CRDT history belongs in the file structure, not reconstructed from the DAG.

## Trump's Iran Ultimatum Tanks Markets

Bitcoin fell below $68k after Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum to Iran ([BeInCrypto](https://beincrypto.com/trump-iran-ultimatum-markets-futures-monday/)). Gold is down 22%, stock futures dropped, and crypto followed. Geopolitics still drive risk-off sentiment faster than any on-chain metric.

## Fidelity Pushes SEC to Integrate Crypto into Traditional Trading

[Fidelity urged the SEC](https://ourcryptotalk.com/news/fidelity-urges-sec-crypto-stock-trading-integration) to let crypto assets trade alongside stocks and bonds in existing systems. If regulators bite, it removes friction for institutional adoption—no separate custody, no siloed infrastructure. Big if.

## Resolv USR Stablecoin Crashes 80% After Security Failure

The Resolv USR stablecoin [depegged and crashed 80%](https://beincrypto.com/resolv-usr-stablecoin-depegs-after-security-failure/) following a security breach. Another reminder that "stablecoin" doesn't mean stable—it means stable until the peg breaks, the oracle fails, or the multisig gets compromised.
